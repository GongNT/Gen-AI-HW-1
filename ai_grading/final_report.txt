## Overall Sentiment

The viewer’s overall response was **positive but initially subdued**. Visually, they appeared tired, neutral, or mildly bored for much of the trailer, especially during the opening. However, their interview responses indicate that they found the trailer increasingly interesting and ultimately compelling. The viewer was particularly drawn to the real history of Tetris, the risks surrounding its international distribution, and the protagonist’s apparent progress toward securing a contract.

Although the viewer said they would watch the full film, their immediate hesitation was practical rather than due to disinterest: the movie was not free to them at the time.

## Key Likes

- **The historical premise:** The viewer repeatedly identified “the Tetris history” as the main reason they would want to watch the film. They said the game’s origins, international conflict, and rise to worldwide popularity all interested them.
- **Escalating stakes and danger:** The viewer found the combination of the contract, business deal, personal risk, and surrounding danger compelling, describing all of these elements as appealing.
- **The protagonist’s apparent progress:** The later part of the trailer suggested to the viewer that the main character was “going to achieve something,” possibly by securing a contract. This made the story more exciting.
- **Growing excitement:** While the beginning felt slow, the viewer said the trailer became exciting later on and that its risks and developing story held their attention.
- **Willingness to watch and recommend:** The viewer said they would watch the whole story if access were available and stated that they would “definitely” recommend the trailer to someone interested in Tetris’s real history.

## Key Dislikes

- **Slow opening:** The clearest criticism was that “the beginning was slow.” This is consistent with the webcam observations from approximately **0–30 seconds**, when the viewer often rested their cheek on their hand and maintained a flat, subdued expression.
- **Limited immediate emotional engagement:** The visual evidence shows little visible excitement during the early and middle portions. From roughly **80–92 seconds**, the viewer remained calm and tired-looking, with only minor posture adjustments and no obvious smile, surprise, or strong emotional reaction.
- **Access limitation:** The viewer wanted to watch the full film but said, “not now, cuz its not free.” This reflects a current availability or cost barrier rather than a negative opinion of the trailer itself.

- **0–3 seconds:** The viewer initially appeared tired or disengaged, resting their cheek on one hand before sitting slightly more upright. This suggests low initial enthusiasm.
- **7–30 seconds:** The viewer continued supporting their face with their hand, occasionally touching their hair or temple. Their expression stayed neutral to mildly weary, matching their later comment that the opening was slow.

This was the clearest positive visual reaction in the recording and aligns with the viewer’s explanation that the trailer was becoming exciting and that the protagonist seemed close to achieving something through a contract.
- **Around 160–170 seconds:** The expression returned mostly to neutral, with the mouth slightly open near 170 seconds. The reaction remained restrained rather than highly enthusiastic, despite the viewer’s positive verbal assessment.

## Summary

The viewer felt **ultimately favorable toward the trailer**, though their visual reactions were mostly neutral and tired. The slow opening reduced their early engagement, but the later escalation, contract storyline, personal risks, and especially the real history of Tetris made the film feel compelling. They would watch the full movie and strongly recommend the trailer, with the only significant barrier being that it was not currently free to access.
